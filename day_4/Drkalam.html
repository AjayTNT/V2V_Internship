Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Personal Details

Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam
Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
He grew up in a small coastal town and helped support his family by selling newspapers.

Family Background:

Came from a humble background — his father was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque.
Despite limited financial resources, his family valued education and spiritual growth.

Education:

Studied physics at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli and aerospace engineering at Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).
He was deeply interested in science and engineering from a young age.

Lifestyle & Beliefs:

Lived a simple life, deeply spiritual, and a strong believer in youth power and education.
He practiced vegetarianism and remained unmarried, dedicating his life to the nation.

Death:

Passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. He died doing what he loved — teaching and inspiring.

Major Achievements

Missile Man of India:
Played a crucial role in India's missile development programs like Agni and Prithvi.
His leadership in DRDO and ISRO made India self-reliant in defense technology.

Key Role in Nuclear Tests:
Was the chief scientific advisor during the 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
His coordination with the military and scientists helped India enter the nuclear club.

11th President of India (2002–2007):
Served as the President of India, known as the "People’s President".
He was admired for connecting with students and encouraging innovation.

Author & Speaker:
Wrote books like "Wings of Fire", "Ignited Minds", and "India 2020".
His writings focus on youth empowerment, science, and national development.

Honors & Awards:
Received Bharat Ratna in 1997, Padma Bhushan in 1981, and Padma Vibhushan in 1990.
He was also honored by many universities and institutions across the world.
