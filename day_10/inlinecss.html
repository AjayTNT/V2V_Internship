My Idol Sports Persons

1. Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli is one of the most celebrated cricketers in the world and a modern-day legend of Indian cricket. Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, he made his international debut in 2008 and quickly rose to fame with his consistent performances and fiery attitude. Known for his aggressive playing style, sharp cricketing mind, and exceptional fitness, Kohli has broken numerous records, including being one of the fastest to score thousands of runs in various formats. He served as the captain of the Indian team across all formats and led the side to several notable victories. Off the field, Kohli is also admired for his discipline, philanthropy, and influence on the younger generation of cricketers.

2. Milkha Singh

Milkha Singh, popularly known as The Flying Sikh, was one of India’s greatest track and field athletes. Born in 1929, he overcame great personal and national tragedy during the Partition of India to become a symbol of hope and perseverance. Milkha represented India in three Olympics and won gold medals in the Asian Games and Commonwealth Games. His most unforgettable performance was at the 1960 Rome Olympics, where he narrowly missed a bronze medal in the 400 meters race. His life story was later adapted into the film Bhaag Milkha Bhaag. Milkha Singh remains a legendary figure in Indian sports history, remembered for his spirit, speed, and contribution to athletics.

3. Mary Kom

Mary Kom is a six-time world champion boxer from Manipur who has made an indelible mark on Indian sports. Born in 1982, she rose to prominence through sheer determination and passion for boxing, despite facing many societal and financial challenges. Known as Magnificent Mary, she is the only woman boxer to win a medal in each of the first seven World Championships and also won a bronze medal at the 2012 London Olympics. Her journey is a story of resilience and excellence, especially as a mother and athlete balancing her personal and professional life. Mary Kom is a role model for countless young women in India and around the world.

4. Neeraj Chopra

Neeraj Chopra is a javelin thrower who brought immense pride to India by winning a historic gold medal at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. Born in 1997 in Haryana, Neeraj developed an interest in javelin throw at a young age and steadily rose through national and international competitions. His Olympic win marked a significant milestone as it was India's first-ever gold in athletics. With a powerful throw of 87.58 meters, he etched his name in history and inspired a new wave of interest in track and field sports in India. Neeraj continues to train hard and is seen as a key figure in India’s future sporting success on the global stage.

5. P. T. Usha

P. T. Usha, often called the Queen of Indian Track and Field, is one of the most iconic athletes in Indian sports history. Born in 1964 in Kerala, she was known for her speed and versatility in sprint and hurdle events. Usha represented India in multiple international competitions, including the Olympics, Asian Games, and World Championships. Her most notable performance came at the 1984 Los Angeles Olympics, where she missed an Olympic medal by just 1/100th of a second in the 400m hurdles. Despite the heartbreak, she continued to dominate Asian athletics and won numerous gold medals. Today, she mentors young athletes and has also served as a Member of Parliament, continuing to contribute to Indian sports.
