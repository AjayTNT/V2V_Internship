1. Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, also known as the "Father of the Nation," played a key role in India's struggle for independence through his philosophy of non-violence and truth. Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, Gandhi led several movements like the Salt March, Non-Cooperation Movement, and Quit India Movement. He believed in peaceful civil disobedience and inspired millions to fight against British rule without using violence. His methods not only united people across India but also influenced civil rights movements around the world. Gandhi's life and teachings remain a symbol of peace, justice, and courage.

2. Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose was a fearless freedom fighter known for his bold leadership and powerful slogan, "Give me blood, and I will give you freedom." Born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, Bose believed that independence should be achieved through force if necessary. He formed the Indian National Army (INA) to fight against British rule with the support of countries like Japan and Germany during World War II. Bose's determination and patriotic spirit inspired many Indians to join the fight for freedom. Though his mysterious death remains a subject of debate, his legacy continues to live on in Indian history.

3. Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh was a young revolutionary who became a national hero for his courage and sacrifice. Born on September 28, 1907, in Punjab, he was deeply affected by the Jallianwala Bagh massacre and British oppression. He joined the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) and was involved in several revolutionary activities against British rule. At the age of 23, he was executed by the British for his involvement in the killing of a British officer and for throwing non-lethal bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly. Bhagat Singh’s bravery and his willingness to die for the country made him a symbol of youthful patriotism.

4. Rani Lakshmibai

Rani Lakshmibai, the Queen of Jhansi, was one of the earliest and most courageous female leaders in India’s freedom struggle. Born in 1828, she fought bravely against the British during the Revolt of 1857. After her husband, the king of Jhansi, died, the British tried to annex her kingdom under the Doctrine of Lapse. Refusing to surrender, she led her army into battle with great bravery and inspired both men and women to rise against British rule. Though she died in battle, her courage and resistance made her a symbol of strength and sacrifice in India’s fight for independence.

5. Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, also known as the "Iron Man of India," played a crucial role in uniting the country after independence. Born on October 31, 1875, in Gujarat, he was a strong supporter of Mahatma Gandhi and led many important movements like the Bardoli Satyagraha. After independence, he became India’s first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister. His greatest achievement was the integration of over 500 princely states into the Indian Union, ensuring a united and strong nation. Patel’s dedication to national unity and administration continues to inspire generations of leaders in India.
